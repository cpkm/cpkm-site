Current research

Explore some of the topics I most recently studied.

Fiber-based ultrafast lasers

Sub-ps laser systems are crucial to all scopes of Ultrafast Science and our understanding of atoms and molecules. I focus on the developement of such fiber-based systems, which benefit from the versitile and robust platform of optical fibers.

High energy amplifiers

High peak-power laser pulses can be used to drive a variety of nonlinear optical phenomena, including optical parametric amplification. I develope both fiber-based and bulk-crystal high energy amplifiers and cavities.

Ultrafast electron diffraction

Ultrafast electron diffraction (UED) is a pump/probe technique used to measure the motion of atoms with sub-Angstrom spacial resolution and sub-ps temporal resolution. The heart of a UED setup is a high power, ultrafast laser.
